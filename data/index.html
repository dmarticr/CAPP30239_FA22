Is Nintendo Falling Behind?

Nintendo is a Japanese video games company founded in 1889 (yes, 1889!), known for creating some of the most popular video games in history, such as Mario, The Legend of Zelda, and Pokémon. Maybe you know, or unsurprisingly, that they distributed the 1st video console in Japan in 1997, but did you know they started off as a playing card company? (and not always for the whole family)

Digging into the company's history, it seems that Nintendo has been a leader in the video game industry for decades. In fact, it was Nintendo who rescued this market after its crisis in the 80s. However, who was once the undisputed industry leader, in recent times has faced new challenges that pushed Nintendo to reinvent itself. Some believe the company has fallen behind, releasing weaker consoles than its competitors.

But success is not measured by console power, and we will see why Nintendo is not only not dying, but still a reference in its industry.

Console vs. Games

From a platform perspective, Nintendo is not behind its competitors in sales. The right graph shows that, based on the release date of the video games, Nintendo's new releases have outperformed those of Sony or Microsoft, whose profits leverage heavily on previously released games.

For a couple of years now, Nintendo has been trying to do something different with their consoles (while the rest of the market orbits around two consoles that do basically the same thing). This strategy has been working out phenomenally for them, focusing on portability and lower prices.

The latter is evident in the pie chart below. Although the comparison is unfair (Nintendo is older), the variety of consoles that Nintendo have offered is greater than Sony or Microsoft (being also cheaper than their counterparts). This has not compromised their leadership since Nintendo has the greatest historical sales, followed by sonny.

Favorable results are also observed when, instead of analyzing video game sales from a console hardware viewpoint, they are broken down according to software production (please press the Publisher button below).

Nintendo is the most profitable publisher followed by Activision and Electronic Arts (which make games for Nintendo too). Also, notice that most of the video games from Sony and Microsoft platforms are not developed by them. This is, although this market is less concentrated than the platforms one, Nintendo still dominates.

Which videogames they do?

Nintendo games are concentrated in 2 main categories: platform games and role-playing games. Of the first, Nintendo accounts for around half of the historical sales of the entire market, while of the second category around a third (left chart).

Here is an interesting fact: platform games are nothing more than those games where you have to move a character from one place to another on the board (like Mario), and this category practically born with a video game from this company in 1981: Donkey Kong, " the first to have a substantial narrative, along with a sense of humor".

Mario debuted in that video game as Jumpman, being one of the first characters to ever actually jump up in a video game (now it seems insubstantial, but that was such a hit at that time!).

Regarding latitudes, the database does not allow us to reach interesting conclusions, most video games are unclassified in this matter. For the classified, total market sales are concentrated in North America and Europe, while Japan Nintendo Sales exceed those of other regions.
